## Answers to the 5 Pre-Build Website Questions

### 1. Who is this website for?

Based on the top performers, the website visitor is **not** a homeowner looking for a socket fitting. It's one of the following decision-makers:

- **Facilities Managers / Building Managers** — responsible for maintaining M&E systems in commercial, healthcare, or education buildings. They need a contractor who can handle planned preventative maintenance, reactive call-outs, and compliance testing.
- **Construction Project Managers / Main Contractors** — looking for a specialist M&E subcontractor for a new-build or refurbishment scheme worth £200k–£100m+. They care about programme certainty, offsite manufacture capability, and HSQE track records.
- **Property Owners / Asset Managers** — evaluating energy performance, EPC compliance, or decarbonisation of an existing portfolio. They need design-and-build capability and long-term maintenance partnerships.
- **Procurement / Estates Directors** (NHS Trusts, universities, local authorities) — must justify spend via accreditations, Modern Slavery statements, and governance documentation.

> **Key insight:** Every top performer segments by *sector* (healthcare, education, commercial, data centres) rather than by *service type*. The visitor self-selects into their world immediately.

---

### 2. What's the ONE action you want them to take?

Across all five companies, the primary CTA is overwhelmingly:

- **"Make an Enquiry" / "Contact Us" / "Get in Touch"**

Specific patterns observed:

- **Lowe & Oliver:** "Make An Enquiry" (links to `/contact`). Also has a "Quick Enquiry" form on the homepage and a "Request A Call Back" option in the footer.
- **Briggs & Forrester:** "How can we help you?" with a smart multi-step routing widget — the visitor selects their enquiry type (new project, supply chain, warranty, recruitment) and is routed to the correct regional division. Phone number prominently displayed: `01604 720 072`.
- **NG Bailey:** "Contact" in the main nav. Case study pages use "View more case studies" as a secondary CTA to keep the visitor in the engagement loop before converting.
- **Gratte Brothers:** "Contact Us" in header nav, with email and phone number always visible in the header bar.
- **TClarke:** "Contact us now" button linking to a general enquiry form.

> **Key insight:** None of them use "Get a Quote" or "Book Now". The commercial electrical space is too complex for instant pricing. The CTA is always a conversation starter — "talk to us about your project." The button copy is low-friction and implies a human response, not an automated one.

### 3. What objections does the visitor have?

The top performers address these objections directly through their site architecture:

| Objection | How the Top 5 Address It | Sections / Pages Used |
|-----------|------------------------|-----------------------|
| **"Can you handle our sector?"** | Every company leads with sector-specific pages (Healthcare, Education, Data Centres, Commercial, etc.) with dedicated project galleries | Sector landing pages, portfolio grids filtered by sector |
| **"Are you actually qualified and compliant?"** | Dedicated accreditation pages listing NICEIC, ECA, ISO 9001, BESA, Gas Safe, JIB. Lowe & Oliver leads with the Royal Warrant. NG Bailey emphasises "Governance and Standards" | `/accreditations`, trust bars, footer badges |
| **"Can you work at our scale?"** | Turnover figures front and centre (B&F: £250m+), employee counts (700+), project capability ranges (£200k–£100m) | Homepage hero stats, about pages |
| **"Have you done something like this before?"** | Extensive named case study portfolios with images, project values, and sector tags. Lowe & Oliver has 10+ full-length named testimonials from directors at Eton College, V&A Museum, NHS | `/case-studies`, `/portfolio`, `/testimonials`, project profile pages |
| **"Are you safe to work with?"** | Dedicated HSQE (Health, Safety, Quality, Environment) sections. NG Bailey uses "Safety First & Foremost" as a standalone nav item. Briggs & Forrester has "Our Safety" | `/safety`, `/hsqe`, dedicated H&S pages |
| **"Can you work where we are?"** | Regional office listings with direct phone numbers. Lowe & Oliver lists Oxford, Southampton, and Cheltenham branches. NG Bailey has nationwide locations page | `/locations`, footer contact details per branch |
| **"Are you a responsible business?"** | Modern Slavery statements, Carbon Reduction Plans, Social Value pages, sustainability commitments | Policy pages, `/sustainability`, `/social-value` |

> **Key insight:** The biggest objection in this space isn't price — it's *"Can I trust you with a multi-million-pound, safety-critical installation in a live hospital/school/data centre?"*. The entire site architecture is built to answer that question.

### 4. What's the vibe?

The consistent vibe across top performers is:

- **Corporate-Professional with Quiet Confidence**

Specific design patterns:

| Element | What the Top 5 Do |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Colour palette** | Dark navy/charcoal backgrounds or white backgrounds with a single brand accent colour. B&F uses a clean green (#2c9942). Gratte Brothers uses dark grey with subtle accents. NG Bailey uses corporate blue. |
| **Typography** | Clean sans-serif fonts throughout. No playful or decorative typefaces. Headings are bold but not oversized. |
| **Imagery** | Large, high-quality project photography — completed buildings, engineering installations, team on-site. Never stock photos of generic electricians. Always real projects. |
| **Layout** | Grid-based, clean white space. Sector/service cards with hover effects. Full-width hero imagery with minimal overlay text. |
| **Tone of voice** | Formal but not stiff. Third-person ("We deliver…", "Our engineers are…"). Avoids jargon where possible. Emphasis on words like *excellence, quality, innovation, trusted, partnership*. |
| **Animations** | Minimal. Subtle hover effects on cards and links. No flashy scroll animations. The content does the talking. |

> **If these websites were people, they'd wear:** A well-fitted navy suit, no tie, polished shoes. Confident handshake. They'd hand you a business card, not a flyer.

> **In Relume terms, the vibe is:** **Premium-Technical** — Dark or white-dominant backgrounds, generous whitespace, data-forward stats (turnover, years, employees), clean grids for projects, and minimal but purposeful micro-interactions.
