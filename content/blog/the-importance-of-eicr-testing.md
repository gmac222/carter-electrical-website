An Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) is an in-depth inspection of your property's electrical systems. For commercial properties, this is not just best practice - it's a legal requirement.

## What is an EICR?

An EICR assesses the safety of the electrical installations in a building. It identifies any damage, deterioration, defects, or dangerous conditions that might pose a risk of fire or electrical shock.

### Legal Requirements

Under the Health and Safety at Work Act 1974 and the Electricity at Work Regulations 1989, employers are responsible for ensuring the health and safety of their employees. This includes ensuring all electrical equipment is safe to use.

Typically, commercial properties must undergo an EICR every **5 years** or at each change of occupancy. However, specific environments may require more frequent testing:

*   **Factories and Industrial Sites:** Often require testing every 3 years.
*   **Offices and Retail:** Every 5 years.
*   **Public Premises (Cinemas, Theatres):** May require testing every 1 to 3 years.

## Benefits of Regular Testing

Beyond legal compliance, regular EICR testing offers several significant benefits:

1.  **Safety:** Protects your employees, customers, and visitors from electrical hazards.
2.  **Insurance Compliance:** Many insurance policies require up-to-date electrical safety certificates. Without one, your policy could be invalidated in the event of an electrical fire.
3.  **Preventative Maintenance:** Identifying potential issues early can prevent costly breakdowns and business downtime.
4.  **Energy Efficiency:** Identifying outdated or inefficient systems can lead to upgrades that save money on energy bills over time.

## What Does an Inspection Involve?

During an EICR, our NICEIC-approved electricians will test:

*   The adequacy of the earthing and bonding.
*   The suitability of the switchgear and controlgear.
*   The serviceability of switches, sockets, and lighting fittings.
*   The type of wiring system and its condition.
*   The presence of adequate identification and notices.
*   The extent of any wear and tear, damage, or other deterioration.
